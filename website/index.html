Welcome to AcreetionOS Mate

AcreetionOS Mate is a user-friendly, privacy-focused Linux distribution based on Arch Linux. It features the MATE desktop environment, XLibre stability, and a curated selection of tools designed for performance and repository sovereignty. It is the ideal choice for users seeking Arch Linux power without the configuration complexity.

Under the Hood

AcreetionOS Mate is more than just a themed version of Arch Linux. We've made several key decisions and customizations to improve the user experience, stability, and performance.

Why XLibre over Wayland?

Notice: We finished migrating to XLibre!

AcreetionOS prioritizes the XLibre foundation (based on X11) over the current industry push for Wayland. We believe that while Wayland is promising, it still lacks the absolute reliability required for professional and production workflows. Many users still face issues with screen sharing, global keyboard shortcuts, and compatibility with specialized hardware or NVIDIA legacy drivers. By sticking with XLibre, we ensure a stable, mature environment where your software "just works" without the configuration headaches currently common in Wayland-based compositors.

Boot Process: Simple & Reliable

We use a combination of bootloaders to ensure a smooth experience. The live environment is booted using systemd-boot and syslinux for maximum hardware compatibility. For the final installation, we use grub, a powerful and widely-supported bootloader. This hybrid approach ensures that AcreetionOS Mate boots reliably on a wide range of hardware, including both modern UEFI systems and older BIOS-based machines.

Filesystem: EXT4 for Stability

We use the EXT4 filesystem by default. While newer filesystems exist, EXT4 is the most mature, stable, and well-tested filesystem in the Linux world. It offers excellent performance for desktop use cases and is incredibly reliable, ensuring your data is safe.

Audio: The Power of Pipewire

AcreetionOS uses Pipewire as its audio server, a modern replacement for PulseAudio. Pipewire is superior in every way: it offers lower latency, better CPU utilization, and seamless support for both consumer and professional audio applications.

Where'd GNOME Go?

The GNOME Edition has been retired. Here's why — and what's next.

Why We Dropped GNOME

The GNOME desktop project has shifted its focus almost exclusively toward Wayland — a next-generation display protocol. While Wayland has promise, it introduces significant compatibility problems for many users: screen sharing breaks in apps like Discord and Zoom, NVIDIA drivers have ongoing issues, and many legacy applications simply don't work correctly under Wayland.

AcreetionOS Mate is built on stability and reliability — we chose XLibre (a modernized X11 foundation) because it "just works" across all hardware without the trade-offs Wayland currently demands. GNOME's hard dependency on Wayland made it incompatible with our philosophy.

What's Replacing It?

We are actively developing AcreetionOS-Mate — a MATE desktop edition that retains the traditional desktop experience while running on XLibre. MATE continues to use the GNOME Shell framework (similar to how MATE does), providing a stable, familiar, and actually working experience.

In the meantime, our flagship MATE Edition and the XLibre Edition are fully supported and actively maintained.
