[!] EXPERIMENTAL STATUS

This build of AcreetionOS Lite is currently in its experimental phase. While stable for many daily tasks, it is a development-heavy release as we bridge the gap towards the XLibre foundation. Use with caution on production hardware.

AcreetionOS Lite is our answer to the modern desktop's descent into corporate-controlled bloat. By utilizing the Mate Desktop Environment, we provide a lightning-fast, highly customizable, and traditional "GNOME 2" style experience for the modern era.

The Lite edition employs a ultra-minimal theme and a carefully curated selection of lightweight binaries. Every component has been stripped of unnecessary overhead while maintaining 100% of the functionality you expect from a daily driver. It's the full power of Linux, without the weight.

Built on the bedrock of Arch Linux, Lite is designed for those who value efficiency, freedom, and the ability to run their OS on anything from a high-end workstation to a legacy machine without sacrificing power.

WHY MATE? THE CORPORATE RESISTANCE

For too long, the Linux desktop has been steered by the whims of Big Corporate. GNOME, once the gold standard of freedom, has transitioned into a rigid, tablet-centric interface dictated by Red Hat and IBM interests. They didn't just change the UI; they stripped away user agency. With the introduction of Libadwaita and the forced removal of desktop icons, traditional menus, and deep customization, GNOME has become a walled garden on an open-source platform. We believe the direction of GNOME is absolute BS.

Many ask: "Why not just use GNOME Classic?" Because GNOME Classic is a lie. It is merely a collection of fragile extensions layered on top of the same bloated, restricted shell. It carries the same corporate baggage and the same technical debt that makes it a nightmare to port and customize.

AcreetionOS Lite chooses MATE because it is the true root of the Linux desktop experience. As a fork of GNOME 2, it preserves the code and the philosophy of a time when the user was in control. It provides that GNOME-Like experience we all fell in love with—the dual-panel layout, the simplicity, the speed—without the "corporate-knows-best" attitude or the forced styling of the modern shell.

Furthermore, the move to MATE is a strategic maneuver. As we look toward the future with our XLibre project, MATE's robust X11 integration makes it significantly easier to port over. While others chase the instability and restrictions of corporate-driven Wayland implementations, we are doubling down on what works for the user.

We aren't just building an OS; we are preserving your right to choose. Reject the bloat. Reject the corporate mandate. Embrace the Mate.
